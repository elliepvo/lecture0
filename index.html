Eggplants

Although purple is the general color that comes to mind when the word eggplant is used, purple is not the only color of eggplants. Like all plants, eggplants come in different cultivars of shape, size, and color. First recorded in 1763, the word itself eggplant refers to the white cultivars, which look like a chicken's egg.

Hence the egg in eggplant.

So what's the difference?

  • Taste wise: the thickness of the skin and the amount of seeds correlates to the bitterness of the plant.
  • The more seeds there are, the more bitter it is.
  • Chinese and Japanese eggplants have less seeds and are, therefore, less bitter.

They are also skinnier. Amongst the eggplant varieties, the Asian horiculture of eggplants dominate with the largest variety of cultivars (in terms of color, shape, and size), compares to the more rounded or egg-shaped plant most people are used to.
